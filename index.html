About me

My name is Douglas GuimarÃ£es , I'm from Brazil and I'm 20 years old. It all started when I was 14 or 15 years old and my mother bought me an older laptop that came with an old version of a Linux distribution called BigLinux. Initially, I tried to install Windows on it, but this laptop didn't even have a hard drive, so I ended up booting BigLinux from a USB stick. While trying to remove it from the USB stick and install it on my old laptop, I discovered (a Brazilian YouTuber) and others who helped me understand Linux.

After my laptop broke, I didn't hear about Linux for a long time. I had other computers, but they all had Windows, and I only used them for basic tasks. In 2019, I received a new laptop from my father, and I was so happy about it... until the hard drive burned out. Instead of telling my father and calling for maintenance, I decided to open it up and try to fix it myself. Did I fix it? Of course not! I admit that I can be a bit clueless sometimes. The only way to keep using it was to install Linux, so I installed Linux Mint.

This experience made Linux so much more interesting to me. I wanted to enter the beautiful world of technology from a developer's point of view - to learn how to make things instead of just using them. I learned Python, JavaScript, Node.js, Bash, and more. Of course, you don't have to be a developer to use Linux, but using Linux and wanting to learn more about it made me who I am today.

Currently, I aspire to be a full-stack developer. I'm a volunteer for the BigLinux distribution (yes, the first distribution I used knowing that it was Linux), and I'm studying information technology while working as a freelancer. You can contact me in the "Contact" section and see my skills in the "Skills" section.
